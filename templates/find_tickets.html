Learn To Find Tickets

Here are ways to find tickets. Press on them to learn more.

- Your Path to the Show

  1. Begin by searching for the show's name in Google.
  2. Navigate to the show’s official website for accurate details and secure purchasing.
  3. On the official website, explore seating options to find the perfect spot. Look for 'Best Value' or 'Discount' tags for savings.
  4. Proceed to checkout. Ensure the website’s security by checking for 'https://' in the URL and a lock icon near the address bar.
  5. Finalize your purchase and receive a confirmation email with your tickets.

- Your Chance for the Spotlight

  1. Review the list of shows offering digital lotteries. Find ones you’re interested in and go to the lottery websites.
  2. Choose a show and click 'Enter Lottery' to begin the process. Submit your entry.
  3. Lottery entries close 24-48 hours before the show. Keep an eye on your email for the results.
  4. Winners are notified a few hours before the show. Check if you've won and proceed to purchase.
  5. If you've won, follow the link in your winner’s email to secure and pay for your tickets.

Rush tickets are offered on the day of the performance, usually when the box office opens. They are sold at a discount, but quantities are limited and seats are often first come, first served. Some theaters have general rush tickets, while others offer Student Rush tickets, requiring a valid student ID.

When a show is sold out, theaters sometimes offer SRO tickets at a discounted rate. These are exactly what they sound like—you'll be watching the performance from a designated standing area. These are usually sold on the day of the performance, directly from the box office.

TodayTix: An app that offers discounted and full-price last-minute tickets. You can enter lotteries, purchase rush tickets, and even get tickets for shows weeks in advance. It’s a convenient, mobile-first approach to getting Broadway tickets.

StubHub, SeatGeek, and Vivid Seats: These platforms sell tickets from individuals or brokers, often at a markup. While you might find tickets to sold-out shows, be prepared to pay above face value. Always use reputable sites to avoid scams.

B’Way Rush: A dedicated website providing information about rush ticket policies, availability, and tips for purchasing rush tickets for Broadway productions.
