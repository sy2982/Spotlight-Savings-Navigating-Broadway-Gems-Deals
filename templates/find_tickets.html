Learn To Find Tickets

Explore the best ways to secure tickets for an enhanced Broadway experience. Click on a section below to learn more about each ticket-buying method and find the right one for your needs.

- Your Path to the Show

  1. Search: Begin by searching for the show's name on Google or on Telecharge.
  2. Official Website: Visit the show’s official website for reliable details and secure ticket purchases.
  3. Best Seats: Choose your seats on the official site. Look for 'Best Value' or 'Discount' tags to save money.
  4. Checkout Security: Ensure secure checkout by verifying the URL and the lock icon in the address bar during payment.
  5. Confirmation: Complete your purchase and receive a confirmation email with your tickets.

- Your Chance for the Spotlight

  1. Review shows offering digital lotteries. Select your favorites and visit their lottery websites.
  2. Enter the lottery by clicking 'Enter Lottery' and submitting your entry.
  3. Lottery entries typically close 24-48 hours before the show. Watch your email for results.
  4. If you win, you will be notified a few hours before the show. Follow the instructions in your winner’s email to purchase your tickets.

Rush tickets are available on the day of the performance, often when the box office opens. They are discounted but limited, and are usually first-come, first-served.

For sold-out shows, SRO tickets are available at a discount in a designated standing area. These are typically sold directly from the box office on the day of the show.

Telecharge: The official site for theatre tickets.

TodayTix: An app that offers discounted and full-price last-minute tickets. You can enter lotteries, purchase rush tickets, and even get tickets for shows weeks in advance. It’s a convenient, mobile-first approach to getting Broadway tickets.

StubHub, SeatGeek, and Vivid Seats: These platforms sell tickets from individuals or brokers, often at a markup. While you might find tickets to sold-out shows, be prepared to pay above face value. Always use reputable sites to avoid scams.

B’Way Rush: A dedicated website providing information about rush ticket policies, availability, and tips for purchasing rush tickets for Broadway productions.
